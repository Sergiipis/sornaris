# Code of Conduct

This project adopts the [Contributor Covenant 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)
as its Code of Conduct. The full text is canonical; this file summarizes
the expectations and points to the enforcement channel for this repository.

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in our community a welcoming, friendly,
and professional experience for everyone, regardless of background,
identity, level of experience, education, or technical preference.

## Expected behavior

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experience levels.
- Accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Focus on what is best for the project and the community.
- Show empathy toward other community members.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal or political insults; demeaning comments aimed at individuals.
- Public or private contact intended to disturb or intimidate another
  participant.
- Publishing others' private information (physical or email address)
  without their explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces: the GitHub repository,
issues, pull requests, discussions, any official community channels
(once announced), and public spaces when an individual is representing
the project.

## Enforcement

Reports of conduct issues can be sent privately by:

1. Opening a GitHub private security advisory at
   `https://github.com/Sergiipis/sornaris/security/advisories/new`
   (use the "Code of Conduct" label in the title), or
2. Contacting the project maintainer directly via the email listed on
   the maintainer's GitHub profile.

All reports will be reviewed promptly. Maintainers are expected to keep
the identity of the reporter confidential.

## Consequences

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including a
private warning, a public warning, temporary suspension from project
spaces, or permanent removal — proportionate to the situation.
